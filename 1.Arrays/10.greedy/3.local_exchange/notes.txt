==================================================
GREEDY — 3. LOCAL EXCHANGE / GREEDY CHOICE
==================================================

Make the locally-optimal choice at each step and argue it can't be beaten globally
(an "exchange argument": swapping toward the greedy choice never worsens the result).
Often a two-pass sweep, a counting formula, or grabbing every positive step.

When to think of this?
- a clear per-step best move that compounds to the global optimum
- two-direction constraints (satisfy each side in its own pass)
- "as many profitable moves as possible"

--------------------------------------------------
Examples of the move
--------------------------------------------------
Candy        : two passes (L->R then R->L), take max of both requirements
Stock II     : sum every positive prices[i] - prices[i-1]
Task Sched.  : frame from the most frequent task: (maxF-1)*(n+1)+ties
