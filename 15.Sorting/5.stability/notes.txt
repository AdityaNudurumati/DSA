==================================================
SORTING / 5. STABILITY CONCEPTS
==================================================

A STABLE sort keeps equal elements in their original relative order. It matters when
you sort by multiple keys in passes, or sort records by one field but want ties to
preserve input order.

Sub-patterns
│
├── Stable Sort               merge sort, counting sort, Python's sorted()/.sort()
├── Unstable Sort              quicksort, heapsort (order of equal keys not guaranteed)
└── Secondary Key Preservation  stable sort by minor key first, then major key

--------------------------------------------------
Key facts
--------------------------------------------------
- Python's Timsort is STABLE.
- Multi-pass: to sort by (major, minor), stable-sort by minor THEN by major.
- A comparison sort can be made stable by tagging each element with its index.
