==================================================
STRINGS / 9. GREEDY ON STRINGS
==================================================

When to think of this?
- Rearrange characters to satisfy a rule (no two adjacent equal)
- Build the lexicographically smallest/largest result
- Partition the string by a greedy boundary

Sub-patterns
│
├── 1.rearrangement/     most-frequent-first (often with a max-heap)
├── 2.lexicographical/   increasing monotonic stack for the smallest subsequence
└── 3.partitioning/      extend a segment to the last index of its chars

--------------------------------------------------
Notes
--------------------------------------------------
- Rearrange: place the highest-count char on alternating slots; feasible iff
  maxCount <= (n + 1) // 2.
- Lexicographically smallest subsequence: increasing monotonic stack, popping while
  a later occurrence of the top still exists (or a removal budget k remains).
- Partition Labels: last[c] = last index of c; extend the window to max(last[...]).
